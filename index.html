The following course description is taken from AP Collegeboard. At the end of the course students could choose to take the AP African American Collegeboard assessment for potential college credit or can register for dual enrollment with our partnership with TCNJ. Course Description: AP African American Studies is designed to be the equivalent of an introductory college or university course in African American Studies and related courses, including Africana Studies, African Diaspora Studies, and Black Studies. AP African American Studies is an interdisciplinary course that examines the diversity of African American experiences through direct encounters with authentic and varied sources. Students explore key topics that extend from early African kingdoms to the ongoing challenges and achievements of the contemporary moment. Given the interdisciplinary character of African American studies, students win the course will develop skills across multiple fields, with an emphasis on developing historical, literary, visual, and data analysis skills. The course foregrounds a study of the diversity of Black communities in the United States within the broader context of Africa and the African diaspora. Course Objectives: Historical Perspective: Explore the origins of African American history from pre-colonial Africa to the present day. Analyze the impact of slavery, the Civil Rights Movement, and contemporary issues on African American communities. Cultural Awareness: Examine the diversity within the African American community, including regional, socioeconomic, and cultural variations. Investigate the influence of African American culture on literature, music, art, and popular culture. Contributions and Achievements: Highlight the significant contributions of African Americans in various fields such as science, technology, literature, politics, and sports. Showcase the accomplishments of African American leaders and trailblazers who have shaped history. Contemporary Issues: Address current social, economic, and political challenges faced by African Americans. Encourage critical discussions on topics such as racial inequality, systemic racism, and social justice.
